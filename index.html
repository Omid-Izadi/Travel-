Street Smarts

The advice that travel YouTubers, local guides and expat bloggers repeat for each city, distilled — scam patterns, etiquette, and the small moves that mark you as prepared. Prague's tips come straight from Honest Guide (Janek Rubeš), the channel every Prague local endorses; the rest reflect the consistent consensus across popular travel channels and local guides.

🇬🇧 London

Tap, never queue

One contactless card each for the Tube — it auto-caps daily. Paper tickets and Oyster queues mark you as new.

CityMapper over Google Maps

It's what Londoners use; it will also tell you when a 'Tube journey' is actually a 4-minute walk (classic Leicester Sq→Covent Garden trap).

Dodge rush hour

7:30–9:30 AM and 5–7 PM the Tube is a crush. Museums at 10, big sights first-slot or near closing.

Free museums still want booking

Even free entry often needs an advance slot in 2026 — reserve the National Gallery & British Museum online.

Don't eat in Leicester Square

London's Times Square. Walk 5 minutes into Soho, or eat at Borough Market. Service charge (12.5%) is already on the bill — no extra tip needed.

Stand on the right

Escalator law. Also: look RIGHT first crossing — traffic comes the 'wrong' way.

Bus 15 is a sightseeing hack

An ordinary £1.75 bus past St Paul's and the Tower — the cheap 'tour bus'.

🇫🇷 Nice / Cannes

Validate or pay the fine

Tram & bus tickets must be validated at the machines — inspectors fine on the spot, no negotiation.

Bolt beats Uber here

Both work on the Riviera; Bolt runs cheaper. But the €1.80 bus/tram network covers nearly everything.

Eat where menus are French-only

Skip anything advertising a 'menu touristique' on the Promenade. The port district and back lanes are where locals eat.

Markets: bring a bag, go early

Cours Saleya (antiques Monday!) or the more local Marché de la Libération. Mornings only.

The beach is pebbles

Water shoes or rent a mattress. The sandy patch is Plage des Ponchettes by Castle Hill.

Dinner starts at 8

Restaurants look empty at 6 and full at 8:30 — book or arrive early-ish. Many kitchens close between services.

Free elevator up Castle Hill

Next to Hôtel Suisse — save the 213 steps for the way down.

🇩🇪 Munich

Validate the paper ticket

Buying isn't enough — stamp it in the little blue box or the fine is €60. The M-zone covers everything you're doing except the castle.

Biergarten law is real

Self-service beer gardens legally allow your own food — buy the drinks, hold the Pfand token for your glass deposit.

Hofbräuhaus early, locals' halls later

Go before 6 PM or it's a scrum. Augustiner am Dom is the quieter local pick two minutes away.

Sunday = closed (except museums for €1)

Shops shut Sundays statewide, but many state museums cost €1 on Sundays. Plan shopping Saturday.

Cash for markets & bathrooms

Viktualienmarkt stalls prefer cash; public toilets run 50 cents. Germans carry coins — do the same.

Don't jaywalk

Locals wait at red lights even at empty streets, and fines exist. When in Bavaria…

Weißwurst before noon

Tradition: the white sausage is a morning food, peeled, with sweet mustard and a pretzel. Afternoon orders get looks.

🇦🇹 Vienna

The coffee house is a reading room

One Melange buys you the table for hours; the aloof waiter is a feature, not an insult. Order by name — Melange, Einspänner — never 'a coffee'.

Pay the waiter, tip in words

Say 'Zahlen bitte', then round up ~10% by naming the total: bill €18.50 → hand €20, say 'stimmt so'.

No turnstiles ≠ free

Vienna's U-Bahn works on trust plus plainclothes inspectors. €2.40 singles or 24 h/48 h passes in the WienMobil app.

Schönbrunn: official site only

Lookalike resellers rank above the real site. Same for concert tickets — never buy from costumed Mozart guys on the street.

The Sachertorte feud is a menu

Sacher's is denser, Demel's fluffier. Pick one; don't feel obliged to like either — the Esterházy torte is better anyway.

Quiet is polite

Keep voices low on transit. A 'Grüß Gott' entering small shops goes a long way.

Käsekrainer safety briefing

Vienna's beloved cheese sausage spits molten cheese on first bite. Stand back; locals will approve.

🇭🇺 Budapest

Never hail a street taxi

The one Budapest rule every guide repeats. Use the Bolt app (Uber is gone) or the official Főtaxi booth. Your trip is tram-and-metro anyway.

Validate, validate, validate

Stamp paper tickets on boarding — inspectors work the M1 line and tram 2 specifically because tourists don't.

Euronet ATMs are a rip-off

Withdraw only at bank ATMs (OTP, K&H, Erste), always in HUF, always decline the conversion offer.

Bath logistics

Bring flip-flops, a towel (rental is ~$14!), and a padlock-friendly attitude. Széchenyi mornings are calm; evenings turn party.

Book Parliament on jegymester.hu only

English tours vanish 2–4 weeks out. Third-party sites sell invalid or marked-up slots.

Cards fine, forints for markets

Small stalls and market halls prefer cash. Learn 'köszönöm' (kuh-suh-nuhm) — it opens faces.

District VII late is fine, VIII quieter

Your flat sits on the VIII/IX ring — the boulevard itself is busy and safe; take a Bolt if returning very late.

🇵🇱 Kraków

Pay in złoty, always

Card terminals will offer euros/dollars — decline DCC every time. Use PKO/Pekao/Santander ATMs, never the gold Euronet boxes.

Milk bars: no tipping, no lingering

Bar mleczny is cafeteria-style — order at the counter, carry your tray, leave when done. It's the point.

Kantor exchange traps on Floriańska

The tourist-strip exchanges run 15–25% below rate. If you must exchange, compare against the mid-rate first.

Auschwitz is a booked-pass system now

Since March 2026 every visitor needs a named online entry pass — no walk-ups. Passports required on entry; keep the day after gentle.

The square empties early morning

Your best photos of Rynek Główny are before 8 AM from your own doorstep. Bugle call plays hourly from St Mary's taller tower.

Jaywalking fines are real

Kraków police actually ticket it — and phone-scrolling while crossing. Use the crossings.

Evening = Kazimierz

Old Town dinner is fine, but the Jewish quarter is where Kraków actually goes at night.

🇨🇿 Prague

Honest Guide rule #1: the exchange trap

'0% commission' hides a terrible rate. Pay by card in CZK, or use bank ATMs (Česká spořitelna, KB, AirBank). NEVER Euronet — their machines push huge withdrawals at ~15% cost.

Honest Guide rule #2: trdelník isn't Czech

The chimney cake is tourist marketing (Janek Rubeš has a whole video). Eat koláče, buchty or a chlebíček instead.

Always decline conversion

ATM or card machine offers 'convert to USD?' — always choose CZK. It's the single biggest silent fee in Prague.

Taxis: apps only

Uber, Bolt or Liftago. Street cabs at the station and Old Town are the last of the old scam economy.

Validate the tram ticket immediately

Inspectors target confused tourists mid-validation. 30 CZK/30 min, 40 CZK/90 min in the PID Lítačka app — no paper needed.

Menus without prices = leave

Old Town Square terraces charge 2–3×. Žabka corner shops (green frog) are the honest minimarkets.

Beer keeps arriving

Traditional pubs refill until you say stop — place a coaster on the glass or say 'zaplatíme' (we'll pay).

🇩🇪 Berlin

Carry actual cash

Berlin is stubbornly cash-first — bakeries, imbisses, even restaurants refuse cards. €50 in pocket daily.

Validate paper; the app self-validates

No barriers anywhere, but plainclothes inspectors show zero mercy. BVG app day ticket AB ~€10.60 covers everything you're doing.

Sunday shutdown

Supermarkets close Sundays; Spätis (corner kiosks) are the exception. Your Museum Island day is Wednesday — correct, since island museums close Mondays.

Don't queue an hour for Mustafa's

Locals' consensus: the famous kebab line isn't worth it. Any busy neighborhood döner — Imren, K'Ups — is as good for half the wait.

Bike lanes are lava

The reddish strip on the pavement belongs to fast cyclists. Standing in it for a photo is the #1 tourist injury-and-insult combo.

Memorial etiquette

No posing on the Holocaust memorial stelae, no balancing walks. Berliners will say something.

Reichstag = passports

Your 8 PM dome slot checks ID against the registration list — bring both passports, arrive 15 min early.

🇩🇪 Hamburg

One ticket, one day

HVV day ticket (~€8.80) covers S-Bahn, U-Bahn, buses AND the harbor ferries. Ferry 62 from Landungsbrücken is a harbor cruise for the price of a bus.

Fischbrötchen: buy where locals stand

Skip the 'super-size cheap' tourist kiosks; Brücke 10 or the fish market stalls turn fish over fast and fresh.

The Elbphilharmonie Plaza is free

Grab the free plaza ticket on site — no need to pay for the 'skip line' online version on a Thursday.

Miniatur Wunderland fills, mornings don't

Your 10 AM slot is the calm window; weekend walk-ups wait 2 h+.

St Michael's at noon

Daily organ recital at 12:00 — if you're nearby, it's ten free minutes of Hanseatic grandeur.

St Pauli 'free entry' isn't

Nightlife touts on the Reeperbahn attach hidden minimums. Not your scene this trip anyway — the 06:09 train says early night.

🇩🇰 Copenhagen

Bike lanes have right of way

The single most repeated Copenhagen warning: never walk, stand, or photograph in the bike lane. When you ride: stay right, signal, no right-on-red.

Pay in DKK, skip the tip

Cards everywhere (many places refuse cash!) — always choose kroner, never home currency. Service is included; rounding up is plenty.

Nyhavn: photograph, don't dine

Quay-side menus run double. Walk two streets back, or hit Torvehallerne/Broens Gadekøkken.

The hotdog is culture

The DOP organic cart by the Rundetaarn is a legitimate Danish institution — everything on it, eat standing.

Donkey Republic for bikes

App-rental oranges everywhere, ~$15/day each — cheaper than hotel rentals. Lights on after dusk (it's the law), lock it even for 2 minutes.

Trust-based transit, real fines

No barriers; inspectors fine 750 DKK. DOT app tickets or a 24 h City Pass Small (~90 DKK) covers your zones.

Smørrebrød has table manners

Fork and knife, never hands — it's practically constitutional.
